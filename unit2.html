Origin of The Universe

      Scientists belive that the universe originated as one single point and expanded rapidly. This theory is called the big bang. They believe the universe is still expanding to this day. The proof they have to prove that it is still expanding is called redshift. Redshift is when the apparent wavelength of an object lengthens and moves to the infrared side of the electromagnetic spectrum. Another peice of evidence supporting the big bang theory is cosmic microwave background (CMB) radiation, belived to be left over from the big bang. CMB is detectable everywhere.

      Just like anything else, stars have a lifecycle. Nuclear fusion occurs in the core of all stars. Two hydrogen atoms combine to form helium. This releases lots of energy. As a main sequence star nears the end of it's life, it's core becomes iron. All the outer layers float away into the universe, spreading all the elements in them. When a supernova occurs, all elements up to iron. All these elements are then released into the universe.
